If an idea hasn't convinced me yet, should I publish it anyway?

The digital garden logic says yes — readers see the *current* state of thinking, not the final version. But readers' time isn't free either. Half-baked thoughts cost something.

Possible answers:

- Mark `status: seedling` and let readers decide whether to read.
- Be explicit in the prose about what I'm uncertain of.
- Allow notes to live in `growing` for months without forcing a finish.
