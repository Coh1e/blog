I keep two notebooks. This site is one of them. The other is a private folder nobody will ever see.

Both are necessary. The public one is for thinking _at_ the world; the private one is for thinking, full stop. Without the public one, the private one becomes a hoarding habit. Without the private one, the public one becomes a performance.

The split:
- **Public** gets ideas that have survived a few weeks. They might still be wrong, but they're _arguably_ wrong.
- **Private** gets the half-formed reaction, the petty grievance, the embarrassing draft. None of that needs an audience.

Material crosses from private to public sometimes. It almost never goes the other way.
