[[en/notes/margin-notes|Gwern's site]] tags many claims with confidence levels — `log`, `likely`, `speculative`, etc. The tag is inline, almost casual. It changes how you read the surrounding sentence.

I've half-adopted this. On notes that are factual claims, I now write a small parenthetical confidence at the end: `(speculative)`, `(strong)`. On essays I don't — the form is different and the confidence is implicit in the tone.

Two things I noticed once I started:

1. Most claims I make are weaker than my prose suggests. Forcing the tag exposes it.
2. The reader trusts the unmarked claims more, because they know I would have flagged them otherwise.

The tag system is small but it's done more for my honesty than any abstract resolution would have.
