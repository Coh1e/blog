> The way to write a book is to actually write a book.
> — Anne Lamott

> I would have written you a shorter letter, but I did not have the time.
> — Pascal

> Premature optimization is the root of all evil.
> — Knuth

> The cost of code is in maintaining it, not writing it.
> — multiple sources

> In a world where everything is amplified, the most subversive thing is to slow down.
> — me, possibly originally someone else

> Done is better than perfect.
> — Facebook's old internal motto. They got many things wrong; this wasn't one.

Each is a correction to a kind of self-deception I fall into every few months. When I see the matching quote, I see the trap.

See [[en/posts/the-discipline-of-finishing|the discipline of finishing]] and [[en/posts/the-cost-of-features|the cost of features]] for two of the patterns I keep falling into.
