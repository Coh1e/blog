The current [[en/notes/reading-in-seasons|reading season]]. Updated as it progresses.

**Anchor:** Christopher Alexander, _The Nature of Order_, vol. 1 (third re-read).

**Supporting:**
- John Berger, "Ways of Seeing" (re-read)
- Jane Jacobs, _The Death and Life of Great American Cities_ (selections)
- Stewart Brand, _How Buildings Learn_ (re-read)
- Two essays from Yi-Fu Tuan's _Space and Place_

**Loose:** miscellaneous shorter pieces I keep coming back to — a few of Hazlitt's essays, a long Gwern piece on architectural writing.

The anchor + supporting set forms a small constellation around _what makes a thing feel alive_. The spring season has been about that question. Next season is open, but probably about something narrower.

If you want recommendations from this list: pick Berger first. Short, transformative, and you can read it on one airplane ride.
