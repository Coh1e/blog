Three pages, longhand, every morning. Stream of consciousness, no stopping. No editing. No judgment.

No one will read it, including you.

## Why it works

Not writing practice — **mental clearing**. Get the low-priority noise out of working memory (last night's dream, office politics anxiety, what someone said) so it doesn't crowd out the day's real work.

## What it isn't

Not a journal (you re-read those). Not a blog (those have audience). Not a Twitter thread (Twitter wants likes).

Morning pages have no audience. That's the most important property.

## Cadence

I do it three or four times a week, not daily. When I notice a "loud brain" day, I do morning pages the next morning.

[[en/posts/the-discipline-of-finishing|The discipline of finishing]] is about writing for publication. This is the inverse: writing that's never published. Both matter.
